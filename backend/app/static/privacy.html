Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 07, 2026

Your privacy is of paramount importance to Bhoomi Digital Justice Platform. This policy outlines how we handle, protect, and utilize the sensitive land record and case information entrusted to us by citizens and legal representatives.

1. Scope of Data Collection

To provide accurate legal tracking, we collect specific datasets including:

  • Personal Identity: Full name, verified email, and contact details for authentication.
  • Geospatial Data: Village names, survey numbers, and property boundaries mentioned in case filings.
  • Legal Documentation: Court orders, FIR copies, and land revenue extracts (RTC/7:12) uploaded for verification.
  • System Metadata: IP addresses and browser logs used for security auditing and non-repudiation.

2. Information Security Standards

Bhoomi employs multi-layered security protocols to safeguard data:

Encryption: All uploaded documents and sensitive fields are encrypted using AES-256 standards both at rest and in transit.

Redaction: Public search results are automatically redacted to hide individual contact details from unauthorized scraping.

3. Purpose of Processing

We process your information solely for the following objectives:

  • Verification: To allow administrators to validate the authenticity of land disputes.
  • Transparency: To provide a transparent, searchable database of ongoing land litigations.
  • Notifications: To update case submitters on verification status and milestones.

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

Bhoomi does not sell or trade user data. Disclosure only occurs under:

  • Legal Mandate: Compliance with court orders or valid law enforcement requests.
  • Public Interest: Displaying non-sensitive case statuses (Village/Survey No.) for public lookup.

5. User Consent and Rights

As a user of Bhoomi, you transition through a tiered consent model:

  • Access: You may request a copy of all data stored against your profile.
  • Erasure: You may request deletion of non-verified submissions. Verified legal records may be retained as per judicial data retention policies.
